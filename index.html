    Vocab of the Month

    01

    Ambivalence

    the state of being uncertain or stuck between two or more options

    Her ambivalence prevented her from immediately signing up for the AP course.

    02

    Corroborate

    v. to provide evidence for; to back up (a claim)

    The note signed by his mother corroborates his claim that he was absent from class yesterday.

    03

    Deference

    n. holding respectful submission to one's judgement, opinion, will, etc.

    Tyler decided to shave off his mohawk in deference to his grandmother.

    04

    Infamy

    n. the state of being well known for a bad quality or deed

    Despite his infamy for being a convicted murderer, Joe still attended church service on Sundays.

    05

    Oust

    v. to remove or force out of (usu. a position or office)

    The rebels ousted the dictator from power.

    06

    Savvy

    adj. having practical intelligence or knowledge

    George is not very savvy when it comes to using public transportation.

    Our Current Favorite Books

    Bud, Not Buddy

    by Christopher Paul Curtis

    The main character, a ten-year-old named Bud, is on a mission to find his father. Bud's mom never told him who his father was, but she left some clues before she passed away: posters of Herman E. Calloway and his famous band, the Dusky Devastators of the Depression. Bud discovers clues in the posters, which he will later piece together to find his father. Once he decides to leave home and find his mystery father, he is unstoppable. This adventurous book, full of laugh-out-loud humor and remarkable characters, hits the high notes of jazz and the deeper tones of the Great Depression.

    And Still We Rise

    by Miles Corwin

    A 2000 nonfiction work, Corwin, a Los Angeles Times reporter narratives a school year spent with twelve senior students in a gifted program at South-Central Los Angeles high school. Throughout the book, the students face many difficulties and must display perserverance to stay in the gifted program. Each student has a different story: one student has to work several jobs after-school, another student is struggling to find a foster home, another ends up getting pregnant, etc. This book describes their courage, achievements, strength, personal crises, setbacks, catastrophes, and triumphs. Will they graduate high school on time?

    On the Shortness of Life

    by Seneca

    This life-changing book is guaranteed to alter your perspective of life. This book focuses on we have control over how influential our life can be. In today's society, humans are prone to wasting their time by mindlessly scrolling through social media or relaxing in front of the TV, instead of challenging ourselves to be better and living our lives to the fullest. Seneca states that we should not put off our enjoyment until tomorrow, because one day, tomorrow will not come.

    Animal Farm

    by George Orwell

    This allegorical novel features a group of farm animals who attempt to overthrow their human farmer and set up a farm to serve in the interest of the farm animals. The plot of the book is very similar to the Russian Revolution of 1917, the death of Lenin, and the rise of Stalin. This book plays with the ideas of rebellion, freedom, and the corruption of power. Orwell was a critic of Stalin and Animal Farm shows what can happen when a cult of personlity develops around one person in power.

    Outliers

    by Malcolm Gladwell

    Outliers looks at how various successful people have reached their goals, and how they got to where they are today. This book gave rise to the 10,000-hour rule, which states that the average human needs 10,000 hours of practice and hardwork to master something. This book also looks into how people become successful due to factors that they had no control. The year they were born, the city where they were born, the family they were born into, can all factor into how one person can become successful in one field, but not in others. If you are interested in the behind-the-scenes of how to achieve great success, then this is the book for you.

    An Epic Swindle

    by Brian Reade

    An Epic Swindle details the takeover of two American businessmen called George Gillett and Tom Hicks. Hicks and Gillett purchased Liverpool FC, an English professional football (soccer) club based in Liverpool. They had huge goals for Liverpool, including building a brand new stadium. Only three years after taking over, they were forced out through a high court injunction after their creditors, RBS, demanded their money back. The book discusses the issues of this financial decision, and the impact towards the businessmen and their fans.>
